About & Methodology

← back to the data

An independent, non-commercial hobby project that turns Florida's public scratch-off data into something you can actually read — odds, remaining prizes, expected value, winners, and retailers.

Where the numbers come from

  • Games, odds & prizes remaining — the Florida Lottery's own public game data feed, refreshed nightly. Every prize tier's odds, total, remaining, and paid counts are theirs, unmodified.
  • Winners — the Florida Lottery's public "Top-Prize Winners with Retailer Information" reports, parsed automatically. Recent winners of $250,000+ may show as anonymous for 90 days under Florida Statute 24.1051, then their names appear.
  • Retailers — the Florida Lottery's public store locator, sampled statewide (13,000+ locations).
  • Home/store locations — mapped to city/ZIP centroids (GeoNames); county populations are U.S. Census estimates.

How the analysis works

  • Expected value (EV/$) is the average payout per $1 for a random remaining ticket. Every game is negative-EV as designed; the best typically sit around $0.80–0.92, and a nearly sold-out game can briefly turn positive on its remaining tickets — we flag those. Figures use advertised face values — most jackpot winners take the cash option at roughly 60–65% of face, so real returns run lower.
  • Estimated tickets are inferred from each tier's odds × prize count (robust to missing tiers). Games with decommissioned/degenerate odds tables are shown without EV.
  • Retailer "lift" is winners ÷ that chain's actual store count, versus the statewide average. It's a sales-volume proxy, not luck — every ticket is independent and random. No store or town is "due."

The Value Score (open formula)

Each on-sale game gets a 0–100 score — unlike other sites, the math is public:

  • 45% — current expected value per $1 (scaled 0.55→0.95)
  • 20% — "profit odds": the chance one ticket pays back more than it cost (design odds, tiers above ticket price; scaled 3%→15%)
  • 20% — freshness: % of total prize value still unclaimed
  • 15% — jackpot health: a 0.5 baseline plus 0.5 × the share of advertised top prizes still out there; zero when none remain (those games are capped at "Avoid")

Bands: 70+ Excellent · 55–69 Good · 40–54 Fair · under 40 Avoid. The score ranks relative value only — every scratch-off remains negative expected value.
